**Missingness Diagnosis**

Missigness Diagnosis refers to the diagnosis of missing values in your DataFrame.

``Examples of Missing Data:``

    'Age': [1, 3, 6, 42, 35, 29, np.nan, 0] (Age is usually not entered as 0)

    'Income': [50000, 45000, 10000, 100000, 344444, np.nan, pd.NA, NAN]

    'Tax': [54.33, 72.89, 55.50, 0.20, na, ]
    
    'Ratings': [Poor, Okay, Average, Good, Great, MISSING, NAN, UNKNOWN, ERROR]

    'Education' : [High School, Bachelors, Masters, PhD, MISSING, NAN, UNKNOWN, Never went to college]

3. **Numerical Diagnosis**

Diagnosis of numerical data or numbers.

``Examples of Numerical Data:``

    'Age': [1, 3, 6, 42, 35, 29]

    'Income': [50000, 45000, 10000, 100000, 344444]

    'Tax': [54.33, 72.89, 55.50, 0.20]

4. **Categorical Diagnosis**

Diagnosis of Categorical data or categories.

``Examples of Categorical Data:``

    'Ratings': [Poor, Okay, Average, Good, Great]

    'Education' : [High School, Bachelors, Masters, PhD]

    'Size' : [XS, S, M, L, XL]

    'product id' : [p1001, p1002, p1002, p1003]

5. **Datetime Diagnosis**

Diagnosis of Datetime data.

E.g:
